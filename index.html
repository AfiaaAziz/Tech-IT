FAQs

Common questions answered

What is web domain and hosting?
A web domain is your website's address (e.g., www.example.com) that people use to access it. It's like your website's name on the internet. Web hosting, on the other hand, is the service that stores your website's files (like HTML, images, etc.) on a server and delivers them to users when they visit your site. Hosting providers manage this server. In short, the domain is your site's address, and hosting is the home where your site lives and is made accessible online. Both are required to make your website live.
Which server is better for websites, Linux or Windows?
Linux servers are preferred for websites using open-source platforms like PHP, MySQL, and WordPress due to stability, security, and being cost-effective. Windows servers are ideal for sites using ASP.NET, MSSQL, or other Microsoft services, typically used in enterprises. For most websites, Linux is a better choice for its flexibility and lower costs, while Windows is suited for Microsoft-based infrastructures.
Google Cloud vs. Amazon AWS: Which is better?
Amazon AWS is more established with a wider range of services and a larger global infrastructure, making it ideal for businesses needing extensive cloud solutions. It offers more mature offerings and flexibility across industries. Google Cloud excels in data analytics, AI, and machine learning, with strong integration into Google's ecosystem. It's known for being user-friendly and offering competitive pricing, particularly for big data projects. The choice depends on specific needs: AWS for broad services and scalability, Google Cloud for data-driven applications and ease of use.
Why is Organic SEO important?
Organic SEO is important because it improves a website's visibility and ranking in search engine results without paid advertising. It builds credibility and trust with users, leading to more sustainable traffic growth. Unlike paid ads, organic SEO creates long-term benefits and reduces reliance on ongoing expenditures. It involves optimizing content, keywords, and technical elements to align with search engine algorithms. Higher organic rankings typically result in better user engagement and conversion rates.
